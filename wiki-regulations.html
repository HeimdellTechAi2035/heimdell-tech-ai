UK Telecom Compliance Wiki

Authoritative reference for UK telecommunications regulatory compliance. Ofcom General Conditions, CCR 2013, PSR 2017, PECR, and UK GDPR requirements explained.

Bottom Line Up Front:

UK communication providers must comply with Ofcom General Conditions C1, C7, and C8, plus CCR 2013 (cooling-off), PSR 2017 (direct debits), and PECR 2003 (data retention). OFCOM-GC-C1

Ofcom General Condition C1: Network Functioning and Interoperability

General Condition C1 establishes requirements for network integrity, fraud prevention, and interoperability for all UK communication providers.

Official Definition:

Communication providers must ensure their networks function correctly, prevent fraud and misuse (including Artificial Inflation of Traffic), and maintain technical interoperability with other UK networks.

Key Requirements:

  • C1.1: Ensure network functions correctly and does not cause harm to other networks
  • C1.2: Implement fraud prevention systems to detect Artificial Inflation of Traffic (AIT)
  • C1.3: Maintain technical specifications for network interoperability
  • C1.4: Report significant security incidents to Ofcom within 24 hours
  • C1.5: Take measures to manage risks to network security

Source: Ofcom General Conditions of Entitlement, Section C1

OFCOM-GC-C7

Ofcom General Condition C7: Consumer Contracts

General Condition C7 establishes consumer contract requirements for residential and small business customers of UK communication services.

Official Definition:

Communication providers must provide clear, standardised contract information before the customer signs, including a Contract Summary and Contract Information document in a prescribed format.

Key Requirements:

  • C7.1: Provide Contract Summary on a durable medium before the contract is concluded
  • C7.2: Include all key information: prices, speeds, contract duration, and termination conditions
  • C7.3: Use the Ofcom-prescribed Contract Summary format
  • C7.4: Inform customers of price changes at least 30 days in advance
  • C7.5: Allow customers to exit without penalty if material terms change

Source: Ofcom Consumer Contract Rules

OFCOM-GC-C8

Ofcom General Condition C8: Switching and Provider Mobility

General Condition C8 ensures customers can switch providers easily and quickly, with automatic compensation for switching delays.

Official Definition:

Communication providers must facilitate switching within one working day using the One Touch Switch (OTS) process. Save Activity is prohibited during the switching window. Automatic compensation applies to switching delays.

Key Requirements:

  • C8.1: Complete residential switching within one working day (One Touch Switch)
  • C8.2: No Save Activity permitted during the switching process
  • C8.3: Provide automatic compensation of Â£4.77/day for delayed switches
  • C8.4: Allow customers to cancel a switch request up to the switch date
  • C8.5: Ensure continuity of service during the switch

Source: Ofcom Switching Rules

CCR-2013

Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013 (CCR 2013)

The Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013 govern distance and off-premises sales, including telesales and online contracts.

Official Definition:

CCR 2013 requires traders to provide pre-contract information on a durable medium, honour a 14-day cooling-off period for distance contracts, and process refunds within 14 days of cancellation.

Key Requirements for Telecom Providers:

  • Regulation 9: Provide pre-contract information before the consumer is bound
  • Regulation 10: Supply the information on a durable medium (paper, email, PDF)
  • Regulation 29: 14-day cancellation period from contract conclusion
  • Regulation 34: Process refunds within 14 days of cancellation
  • Regulation 36: If service begins during cooling-off at customer's request, they pay proportionally

Source: The Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013

PSR-2017

Payment Services Regulations 2017 (PSR 2017)

The Payment Services Regulations 2017 govern how businesses collect payments, particularly relevant for telecom providers using Direct Debit.

Official Definition:

PSR 2017 establishes rules for payment service providers and businesses collecting payments. Providers must obtain explicit consent, provide payment service information, and refund unauthorised transactions within one business day.

Key Requirements for Telecom Providers:

  • Regulation 66: Obtain explicit consent before collecting Direct Debit payments
  • Regulation 45: Provide payment service information before the consumer is bound
  • Regulation 76: Allow customers to cancel payment mandates at any time
  • Regulation 75: Refund unauthorised transactions within one business day
  • Regulation 92: Notify customers of payment collection at least 14 days in advance

Source: The Payment Services Regulations 2017

PECR-2003

Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 (PECR)

PECR governs how communication providers handle electronic communications data, marketing communications, and cookies.

Official Definition:

PECR establishes rules for electronic communications privacy, including traffic data retention limits, marketing consent requirements, and security obligations. Enforced by the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

Key Requirements for Telecom Providers:

  • Regulation 7: Traffic data must be erased or anonymised when no longer needed (typically 12 months max)
  • Regulation 22: Marketing communications require consent (soft opt-in for existing customers)
  • Regulation 5: Implement appropriate security measures for electronic communications
  • Regulation 6: Obtain consent for cookies and similar technologies
  • Regulation 24: Maintain accurate subscriber directories

Source: ICO Guide to PECR

UK-GDPR-ART-22

UK GDPR Article 22: Automated Decision-Making

Article 22 governs how organisations can use automated decision-making systems, particularly relevant for AI-powered compliance tools.

Official Definition:

Data subjects have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing, including profiling, which produces legal effects or similarly significantly affects them. Exceptions exist where explicit consent is given or where there are suitable safeguards.

Key Requirements for AI Compliance Systems:

  • Article 22(1): High-impact automated decisions require human review (Human-in-the-Loop)
  • Article 22(2)(c): Explicit consent can authorise automated decisions if safeguards exist
  • Article 22(3): Controller must implement measures to safeguard data subject rights
  • Article 13(2)(f): Provide meaningful information about the logic involved in automated decisions
  • Article 15(1)(h): Data subjects can request explanation of automated decision logic

Source: ICO Guidance on Automated Decision-Making

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about UK telecom compliance requirements.

What happens if I breach Ofcom General Conditions?

+
Ofcom can impose fines of up to 10% of relevant turnover for General Conditions breaches. They can also issue enforcement notifications, require remedial action, and in serious cases, revoke your authorisation to provide communications services.

Do CCR 2013 cooling-off rules apply to business customers?

+
CCR 2013 primarily protects consumers (individuals acting outside their trade/business). However, some provisions also protect sole traders and small partnerships. Ofcom GC C7 extends contract protections to small businesses (10 employees or fewer).

How long must I retain call data records under PECR?

+
PECR requires traffic data to be erased or anonymised when no longer needed for billing or traffic management. In practice, 12 months is the typical maximum for retained call data. Longer retention requires a lawful basis under UK GDPR.

What is One Touch Switch (OTS)?

+
One Touch Switch is Ofcom's process allowing residential customers to switch broadband providers within one working day. The gaining provider handles the entire process; the customer doesn't need to contact their old provider. Implemented under GC C8.
