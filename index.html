Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions About Post-Sale Compliance

How does cooling-off compliance work for telecom resellers?

+
Customers have 14 days to cancel off-premises telecom sales under UK law. Under the Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013, customers who purchase telecom services away from business premises have a 14-day cooling-off period. The Silver Protocol ensures every customer receives a clear verbal explanation of their cancellation rights during a recorded verification call, followed by instant written confirmation via SMS and email. This creates a timestamped, provider-ready audit trail proving compliance — eliminating the most common cause of commission clawbacks.

How does The Silver Protocol protect my commission?

+
It creates a compliance certificate for every sale, removing the grounds for clawbacks. Commission clawbacks typically occur when a provider disputes the validity of a sale — often citing missing cooling-off confirmation, unverified identity or absent contract acknowledgements. The Silver Protocol independently verifies every sale after it is logged, producing a compliance certificate that includes ID confirmation, key term acknowledgement, cooling-off confirmation, Direct Debit validation, a voice recording reference and a timestamp. This certificate satisfies provider audit requirements and removes the grounds for disputed commissions.

What happens during a provider audit?

+
You present compliance certificates that contain all required evidence instantly. When a provider such as Virgin Media, O2 or Sky audits your sales, they request evidence that each customer was properly informed, consented explicitly and received the required disclosures. The Silver Protocol generates a provider-ready compliance certificate for every sale, stored alongside indexed voice recordings and digital delivery logs. During an audit, you present certificates that contain all required verification data — turning a high-risk review into a straightforward evidence exercise.

How quickly does post-sale verification happen?

+
Under 5 minutes — triggered instantly when a sale is logged. The Silver Protocol is triggered immediately after a sale is logged. The customer receives a verification call within minutes of sign-up. Identity validation, verbal confirmation of key terms, cooling-off explanation and recording are completed during a single managed interaction. SMS and email confirmations are dispatched instantly upon completion. The compliance certificate is generated and pushed to your CRM in real time.

Is The Silver Protocol compliant with UK telecom regulations?

+
Yes — built around Ofcom General Conditions, CCR 2013 and PSR 2017. The Silver Protocol is designed around the regulatory framework governing UK telecom sales, including Ofcom’s General Conditions of Entitlement (particularly GC C7 on contract information and GC C8 on switching), the Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013 covering cooling-off rights, and the Payment Services Regulations 2017 governing Direct Debit mandates.

Do you integrate with CRM systems?

+
Yes — certificates and compliance data push directly into your CRM automatically. The Silver Protocol is designed to integrate with leading telecom CRM platforms. Once verification is complete, the compliance certificate — including all validation data, recording references and timestamps — is pushed directly into the relevant CRM record. This ensures your sales pipeline reflects verified compliance status without manual data entry.

What if a customer disputes the sale later?

+
The Silver Protocol provides timestamped voice recordings, delivery logs and certificates as permanent proof. If a customer claims they did not agree to the contract, were not informed of the cooling-off period, or never received the terms, The Silver Protocol provides irrefutable evidence to the contrary: a timestamped voice recording of explicit verbal confirmation, SMS and email delivery logs with timestamps, and a compliance certificate summarising every verification checkpoint. This evidence is indexed and retrievable on demand — serving as a permanent defence against retrospective disputes.

100 or more monthly sign-ups — the threshold where compliance gaps cause significant leakage. The Silver Protocol is designed for telecom resellers processing 100 or more monthly sign-ups.
